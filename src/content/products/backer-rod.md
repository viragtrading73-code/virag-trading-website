  - question: What is backer rod used for?
    answer: Backer rod is used to fill deep gaps and expansion joints before applying a sealant. It controls the depth of the sealant, ensuring it performs optimally while preventing the sealant from adhering to the bottom of the joint.
  - question: Why is a closed-cell foam backer rod preferred?
    answer: Closed-cell polyethylene foam is non-absorbent and moisture-resistant, meaning it won't wick water into the joint. It also prevents air bubbling in the sealant during curing.
  - question: How does it improve sealant performance?
    answer: By preventing three-sided adhesion, the backer rod acts as a bond breaker. This allows the sealant to stretch and compress freely with the movement of the building materials without tearing.

## Product Description

Backer Rod is a flexible closed-cell foam rod used as a backing material in expansion joints, construction gaps, glazing applications, and sealing projects. It is installed before sealant application to control sealant depth, reduce sealant consumption, and improve joint performance.

Its non-absorbent structure prevents moisture penetration while helping create a proper bond breaker for effective sealant movement and durability.
